# Buckland

Buckland is across the river from the Marish in eastern Shire. There is
constant traffic over the Brandywine Bridge between these.  Buckland nominally
in Bree-land but is indepedent and considered the eastern border of the Shire.

## Landscape

- Brandy Hall stocks up on cider, beer, and root vegetables.
- Bucklanders prepare for autumn festivals.
- Trees turning golden & red – apple trees heavy with fruit—perfect for cider
  making.
- Local fields harvested by Hobbits for grain and storing up for Winter.
- Mushrooms eagerly sought across the Great East Road or further. Hobbits love
  mushrooms.

## Weather and climate

Mild, temperate climate similar to England’s West Midlands or the countryside
of Wales in early autumn.

- Late September: Cooler nights, misty mornings, trees are changing color.
- Daytime: ~13–18°C (55–65°F) – Mild and comfortable with lingering warmth.
  Cool but pleasant.
- Nighttime: ~5–10°C (41–50°F) – Cooler evenings, but no frost yet. Chilly
  especially near the Brandywine River. Encourages fireplaces and warm meals.
- Humidity: Moderate, due to the proximity of the Brandywine River.
- Wind: Light to moderate breezes from the west or southwest. (Elves see this
  as a sign of favor from Valinor in the West.)
- Rainfall: Light drizzles, sometimes heavy downpours, occasional
  thunderstorms.
- Fog: Strong mist along the Brandywine River and marshes, especially in the
  early morning.
- Storms: Occasional autumn storms, but nothing severe, though yesterday there
  was a heavy downpour.

## Hazards

- Mud from recent rains – Roads are slick and difficult for carts and wagons.
- Morning mist along the Brandywine – Hard to navigate, easy to miss paths
  near there. Crossing the river may be tricky.
- Chilly river breezes – A damp wind off the Brandywine makes evenings feel
  colder. Sleeping out-of-doors will be unpleasant and might draw attention.
- Brandywine River water levels steady, though if there is another downpour,
  there may be flooding along the banks.
- Old Forest remains eerie, its trees starting to change color, though the
  deeper woods are unnaturally dense year-round as you spotted from traveling
  the Great East Road.

## Local events

### River-lantern Festival

The festival is **tomorrow night**.

- You all know how to boat, right?
- Festival tables filled with autumn harvest for all (including visitors) to
  enjoy.
- Boat on the river at night with garlands, autumn leaves, and mushrooms lit
  by candle.
- Cross the river. The final ferry of the night carries only lanterns,
  drifting without a guide.
   - It is believed that spirits of the river folk might guide the ferry on
     its way
   - Some claim to have seen faint figures watching from the water—but that’s
     just old Hobbit superstition, right?
- Things you overhear in Buckland:
   - _Sometimes the lanterns drift against the current... don't fall in!_
   - _A boat vanished last year—found empty downriver the next morning._
   - _Boats are quite tricky enough for those that sit still without looking
     for the cause of trouble_

### Brandywine Harvest Feast

The first apple pressing is **two nights away**.

- A big community gathering in Brandy Hall, open to all Hobbits (and friendly
  travelers).
- Apple cider pressing & tasting.
- Barrel-rolling races.
- Storytelling contests in Brandy Hall’s main hall.
- Dance & music by the riverbank.

### Buckland Night Watch

Apprehending darker nights ahead is **three nights away** if you stay and gain
trust of the Watch with your observations.

- A traditional patrol along the Hedge before autumn deepens.
- Led by Shiriffs and older Brandybucks, some of whom claim they’ve seen
  the trees move.
- Rumors heard in Buckland when you ask or overhear:
  - _Someone saw flickering lights in the Old Forest._
  - _The Brandywine rose strangely high last night._
  - _Old Rory Brandybuck swears he saw a fox walking on two legs._
  - _Strange dreams in Brandy Hall lately—visions of a golden tree._
