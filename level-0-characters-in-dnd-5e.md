# Level 0 Characters in D&D 5e

You&rsquo;ve had an unusual upbringing or surprising turns of fortune, and
believe adventuring may be for you. The essence of a Level 0 starting
character is dipping your toes in new directions whether by choice, suggestion
of a patron or friend, poor personal choices, or outside forces.

## Creating a Level 0 Character

Use the template below (_Adventurous Commoner_) to build a level 0 character.
Add to that:

- Choose a _Background_.
- Choose a _Species_.
- Choose _Ability Scores and Modifiers_.

You gain all Background and Ability Score benefits and Species features not
tied to character level. These choices carry over to level 1. You start with a
_Proficiency Bonus_ of +2 (same as level 1) reflecting your competence and
pre-adventuring experience.

### Limitations for Level 0 Characters

You begin with a single **1d8 Hit Die**, which you can expend during a _Short
Rest_ to regain Hit Points equal to a roll with this die + your Constitution
modifier.

You begin with no Spell Slots. If you have spellcasting capabilities from your
Species or Background, you may only cast Cantrips until you reach level 1.
You retain those features and can pre-select your 1st-level spells, but they
are locked and unusable for now.

## Leveling Up to 1

When you reach Level 1, your pre-adventuring life ends and your heroic path
begins. Pick your starting 1st-level Class and apply the following
adjustments:

- **Hit Points.** Replace your _Hit Points_ with those of your level-1 Class.
  Subtract damage (if any) below your current maximum hit points. Your level-1
  _Hit Points_ are always greater than your level-0 maximum.
- **Hit Dice.** Replace your 0 level Hit Die (d8) with your Class Hit Die
  (e.g., a d6 for Wizards, a d10 for Fighters).
- **Proficiencies.** Add your Class&rsquo;s Saving Throw, Weapon, and Armor
  proficiencies, as well as any bonus Skills, Tools, or Languages it provides.
- **Equipment.** You add starting Class equipment not duplicating your current
  equipment.
- **Magic.** You unlock any 1st-level spell slots or Class features listed
  under Level 1.
- **Traits.** You permanently lose your Level 0 traits (_Foolhardy
  Adventurer_, _Narrow Escape_, and _Newcomer&rsquo;s Luck_).

### Traits

***Foolhardy Adventurer.*** Once per _Long Rest_ you may reroll one _D20 Test_
immediately after you roll the d20 but before the DM determines the outcome.
You lose this trait when reaching Level 1.

### Actions

***Narrow Escape.*** Once per _Short Rest_, when you take the _Dash_ action on
your turn, you can double the extra movement it provides for that turn. You
lose this trait when reaching Level 1.

### Reactions

***Newcomer&rsquo;s Luck.*** Once per _Long Rest_, as a _Reaction_ when a
creature you can see within 60 feet is about to make a _D20 Test_, you can
impose _Disadvantage_ on the roll. You lose this trait when you reach Level 1.

## Examples

### The Plucky Farmhand

*A surprisingly durable Human kid who is handy with livestock and naturally
lucky, destined to become a Fighter.*

* **Ability Array Chosen:** Str 14, Dex 12, Con 15, Int 8, Wis 13, Cha 10
* **Human Species Benefits:** *Versatile* trait (grants an extra Origin Feat:
  **Lucky**), *Skilled* trait (Survival proficiency), and *Resourceful* trait
  (starts each day with _Heroic Inspiration_).
* **Farmer Background Benefits:** +2 Constitution, +1 Strength. Animal
  Handling and Nature proficiencies. Carpenter&rsquo;s Tools. Origin Feat:
  **Tough**.

### Origin Feats & Species Traits

***Resourceful.*** Josias gains _Heroic Inspiration_ whenever he finishes a
_Long Rest_. <br/>
***Tough (Feat).*** HP maximum is increased by 2 (included above). <br/>
***Lucky (Feat).*** Josias has 3 Luck Points. He can spend a point to give
himself _Advantage_ on a _D20 Test_, or spend one as a _Reaction_ to impose
_Disadvantage_ on an attack roll made against him. Regained after a _Long
Rest_.

### Level 0 Traits

***Foolhardy Adventurer.*** Once per _Long Rest_, immediately after rolling a
_D20 Test_ but before the outcome is known, Josias can reroll the die. <br/>
***Narrow Escape.*** Once per _Long Rest_, when taking the _Dash_ action,
Josias can double the extra movement provided. <br/>
***Newcomer&rsquo;s Luck.*** Once per _Long Rest_, as a _Reaction_, Josias
can impose _Disadvantage_ on a _D20 Test_ he can see within 60 feet before the
die is cast.

### The Street Urchin

*A quick-fingered Wood Elf lookout who survives on razor-sharp instincts,
destined to become a Rogue.*

* **Ability Array Chosen:** Str 8, Dex 15, Con 12, Int 10, Wis 13, Cha 14
* **Elf Species Benefits:** *Darkvision* (60 ft), *Keen Senses* (Insight
  proficiency), *Elven Resilience* (Advantage vs Charm), and *Elven Lineage*
  (Wood Elf: grants +5 Speed and the *Druidcraft* cantrip).
* **Urchin Background Benefits:** +2 Dexterity, +1 Wisdom. Sleight of Hand and
  Stealth proficiencies. Thieves&rsquo; Tools. Origin Feat: **Alert**.
