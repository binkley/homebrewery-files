# Level 0 Characters in D&D 5e

You&rsquo;ve had an unusual upbringing or surprising turns of fortune and find
adventuring for you. A level 0 character dips your toes in new directions
whether by choice, suggestion of a patron or friend, poor personal choices, or
outside forces.

{{note

This document is homebrew for 5.5e sticking close to PHB. There are no
official rules for Level-0 characters. Other ideas include:
- _[A Guide to Playing Level 0

- _[Starting From Scratch: A Guide to Level 0 Play for D&D

## Creating a Level 0 Character

Use the _Adventurous Commoner_ template to build a level 0 character following
standard PHB character creation except for picking a Class.

- Choose a _Background_.
- Choose a _Species_.
- Choose _Ability Scores and Modifiers_.

You gain all Ability Score, Background and Species benefits not tied to
character level. You start with a _Proficiency Bonus_ of +2 just as level 1
reflecting your competence and pre-adventuring experience.

### Limitations for Level 0 Characters

You begin with a single **1d8 Hit Die**, which you can expend during a _Short
Rest_ to regain _Hit Points_ equal to a roll with this die + your Constitution
modifier.

You begin with no Spell Slots. If you have spellcasting capabilities from your
Species or Background, you may only cast Cantrips until you reach level 1.
You retain those features and can pre-select your 1st-level spells, but they
are locked and unusable for now.

The **Tough** origin feat grants a flat +2 to _Hit Points_, and **Dwarven
Toughness** grants a flat +1 _Hit Points_. These to not carry over to level 1.

{{note
**Player Tip**
When you can use a Skill and Tool proficiency together, you get _Advantage_ on
your _D20 Test_ roll. This does not depend on your level.
}}

### Leveling Up to 1

When you reach Level 1, your pre-adventuring life ends and your heroic path
begins. Pick your starting 1st-level Class and apply the following
adjustments:

- **Hit Points.** Replace your _Hit Points_ with those of your first Class.
  Subtract any current damage. (_Hit Points_ for level 1 are always greater
  than for level 0).
- **All Other Features.** You carry no feature to level 1. You only carry your
  posessions. You gain all Background or Species features as if you had
  started at level 1.
- **No Level 0 Features.** You permanently lose your Level 0 traits
  (_Foolhardy Adventurer_, _Narrow Escape_, and _Newcomer&rsquo;s Luck_).

*Small or Medium Humanoid, Any alignment*

**Proficiency Bonus**   :: +2

**Tool proficiencies**  :: Background and Species
**Equipment**           :: Background and Species
**Resistances**         :: None or Species
**Immunities**          :: None or Species
**Senses**              :: Passive Perception (10 + Wisdom bonus) and Species
**Languages**           :: Common + Background and Species

### Traits

**Foolhardy Adventurer.** Once per _Long Rest_ you may reroll one _D20 Test_
immediately after you roll the d20 but before the DM determines the outcome.
You lose this trait when reaching Level 1.

### Actions

**Narrow Escape.** Once per _Short Rest_, when you take the _Dash_ action on
your turn, you can double the extra movement it provides for that turn. You
lose this trait when reaching Level 1.

### Reactions

**Newcomer&rsquo;s Luck.** Once per _Long Rest_, as a _Reaction_ when a
creature you can see within 60 feet is about to make a _D20 Test_, you can
impose _Disadvantage_ on the roll. You lose this trait when you reach Level 1.
}}

## Examples

### Human Farmer to Fighter

*Josias is an stout aware Human handy with livestock destined to be a
Fighter.*

* **Human:** Extra Origin Feat: **Alert**.
* **Farmer:** **+2** Constitution, **+1** Strength. Origin Feat: **Tough**.
* **Ability Score Picks:** Str 14, Dex 12, Con 15, Int 8, Wis 13, Cha 10

*Medium Humanoid (Human), Neutral Good*

{{vitals

**HP** :: 9 (4 +3 Constitution bonus +2 Tough feat)

**Proficiency Bonus** :: +2
**Skill proficiencies** :: Animal Handling (Farmer), Nature (Farmer), Survival (Human)

### Origin Feats & Species Traits

**Resourceful.** Josias gains _Heroic Inspiration_ after a
_Long Rest_. <br/>
**Tough (Feat).** Josias increases _Hit Point_ maximum 2 per
level. <br/>
**Alert (Feat).** Josias can swap _Initiative_ with an ally when you both are
not _Incapacitated_.

### Level 0 Traits

**Foolhardy Adventurer.** Once per _Long Rest_, immediately after rolling a
_D20 Test_ but before the outcome is known, Josias can reroll the die. <br/>
**Narrow Escape.** Once per _Short Rest_, when taking the _Dash_ action,
Josias can double the extra movement provided. <br/>
**Newcomer&rsquo;s Luck.** Once per _Long Rest_, as a _Reaction_, Josias can
impose _Disadvantage_ on a _D20 Test_ he can see within 60 feet before the die
is cast.
